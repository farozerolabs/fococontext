# Code of Conduct

FocoContext is built for people who care about reliable knowledge systems,
open integrations, and clear engineering. We want the project to stay direct,
useful, and respectful for contributors, maintainers, users, and downstream
teams.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful when discussing bugs, tradeoffs, roadmap decisions, and design
  disagreements.
- Keep feedback specific and actionable. Critique ideas, code, tests,
  documentation, and product behavior.
- Assume contributors have different levels of context. Ask clarifying
  questions before escalating a disagreement.
- Share enough evidence for others to reproduce or evaluate a claim.
- Respect privacy. Do not publish secrets, private documents, customer data,
  exploit details, or personal information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention.
- Publicly posting private information without permission.
- Deliberately derailing issues, discussions, pull requests, or maintainer
  review.
- Sharing exploit details in public channels instead of following
  [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces such as GitHub issues,
discussions, pull requests, reviews, documentation, examples, and community
channels connected to FocoContext.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close discussions, block users, or restrict
participation when behavior harms the project or community. Serious security or
abuse reports should be sent through the private reporting path described in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

Maintainers will review reports with context, keep sensitive information
private when possible, and choose actions that protect contributors and the
project.
